Ballerina Mogau Ntsoane

Mother Of All Orishas

Ballerina Mogau Ntsoane was born in the year 1991 on the 18nth of July. she was raised by her grandmother for 3years because her mother was steal very young and her father recited in Johannesburg because he was working to support her family. she only moved to live with both her parents at the age of 5. Her mother was a very phenominal person who managed to take her to a private school to get the best education even though she earned a basic salary of R 800.

During her chilhood years she witnessed a lot of abuse in her household. Her father was not the person he knew anymore, he was abusing her mother. This experience led to something no one could ever think it could happen to a teenage girl, she started losing hair because of depression and a mental disorder. Her mother got sick along the way and eventually passed on. All this events caused a strain in her life but all this had a positive outcome because this is were she began her journey of writing.

To this day she has accomplished a lot because of her passion for changing the society she lives in and making the world a better place. she has shown the love fo her community through volonteering and being part of organisations such as LoveLife, Northern Illinois Food Bank and Feed My Starving Children. She has also accomplished a lot for herself because she has certificates in Management Of Technology and innovation, Facilitation, Mass Communication, Broadcast Journalism. through her writing journey she has written a couple of short stories and articles but one of the best and published short story is Dirty Laundry which and The Bald Honey Tales.
